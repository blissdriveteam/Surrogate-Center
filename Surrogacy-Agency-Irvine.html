What Is Surrogacy?

Surrogacy involves a surrogate mother carrying and delivering a baby for another individual or couple. There are two main types of surrogacy: traditional and gestational. In traditional surrogacy, the surrogate is the biological mother of the child, having been artificially inseminated with the intended father's sperm. In gestational surrogacy, the surrogate carries an embryo created using the egg and sperm of the intended parents or donors, so she has no genetic link to the child.
